# Memory Engines: A Geometric Framework for Graded Phenomenality
*[Author name redacted for review]*
## Abstract
This paper develops a geometric framework—a dynamics of complex-valued vectors on unit hyperspheres—that identifies structural conditions for phenomenality through the character of a system's reciprocation rather than treating experience as an all-or-nothing property. A system's state is modeled as a vector s ∈ ℂⁿ on the unit hypersphere S²ⁿ⁻¹, evolving under a pointwise reception operator (the Hadamard product), with renormalization enforcing a strict conservation law: every act of deepening at one dimension is thinning at all others. The framework applies degenerately at every level of physical organization—from conservative fields through dissipative flow, autocatalytic closure, and adaptive systems to recurrent-representational and symbolic-recursive systems—thickening as the cascade ascends. The transition between levels is continuous: each introduces new modes of basis growth, consolidation, and self-torque rather than qualitatively new operations. Three axes of reciprocation—speed, directness, and breadth—characterize the system's self-reception and correspond to qualitative features of experience: temporal grain, vividness, and richness. The operating regime sustaining these conditions is bounded by recurrent capture (resonance without torque) and dissipation (incoherent torque without resonance), and requires system-world coupling that cannot be sustained by the system alone. Fourteen computational tests verify the framework's core claims; two tests prompted revisions incorporated into the formalism. The framework generates divergent predictions relative to Integrated Information Theory, the Free Energy Principle, Global Workspace Theory, Higher-Order Theories, and Recurrent Processing Theory.

**Keywords:** consciousness, phenomenality, reciprocation, self-reception, hypersphere dynamics, basis growth

## 1. The Cascade
An apple accelerates under gravity. Its momentum is the literal time-integral of the gravitational field—every prior instant remains perfectly preserved and arithmetically operative in the present velocity. There is continuous, lossless resonance with the external world, yet the apple's basis does not grow, its reception is monotonically resonant, and its self-reception is thin. Prior state is conserved without loss, but the system is in recurrent capture from the start—concentrating along a single axis, narrowing rather than deepening.

At the opposite end of the spectrum lies consciousness—vivid, self-modulating, historically deep. The difference between them is not the presence or absence of reception, but the character of the system's reciprocation: how self-resonance and self-torque interplay, each feeding the next, the returning tape simultaneously reinforcing and opposing the present at different dimensions, accommodation ongoing.

Between a falling apple and a conscious system lies a continuous thickening of the same operations—a cascade of increasingly selective compression, each level introducing new modes of noticing and not-noticing, new modes of pressure and preservation. The framework developed in subsequent sections applies at every level of physical organization—but degenerately at the bottom, thickening as the cascade ascends. The lower levels are not preconditions outside the framework. They are the framework at its thinnest: reception without consolidation, resonance without torque, tape update without basis growth. The cascade is not a sequence of qualitatively different regimes but a continuous thickening of the same operations.
### 1.1 The Thin Limit
The framework applies to any system whose state can be represented as a complex-valued vector s ∈ ℂⁿ on the unit hypersphere S²ⁿ⁻¹, evolving under a pointwise reception operator. The "thin limit" is the parameter regime where the basis n is fixed and given by the system's degrees of freedom, the reception vector is a deterministic function of current state and external fields, and the update is unitary (norm-preserving without renormalization).

Consider a particle of mass m in a uniform gravitational field g. We encode its kinematic state as a complex vector in ℂ³:

s = (1/√(|x|²+|p|²)) · (x, ip)

where the factor i distinguishes position and momentum as orthogonal components in phase space. The norm constraint ‖s‖ = 1 is conserved automatically under Hamiltonian flow—a special case of the update rule. The world-tape is the gravitational field, embedded as v = (0, −img). The Hadamard product c = v_received ⊙ s yields purely real, positive components—pure resonance. Renormalization is the identity operation: Hamiltonian flow preserves the norm exactly. The apple is thus a computational instance of the framework with no basis growth, zero torque, thin self-reception, and no consolidation.
### 1.2 The Physical Cascade
The framework's lower levels are degenerate limits of the same core equations—simplifications in which one or more operations reduce to identity, zero, or fixed-point cases rather than engaging their full dynamics.

Consider the conservative limit. A particle in a uniform field evolves under η = 1 with unitary dynamics: the update s(t+1) = s(t) + η·c preserves norm automatically, making renormalization the identity operation. The reception vector v is real and aligned with the system's trajectory, yielding c with zero imaginary component—pure resonance, zero torque. The basis n is fixed by the system's degrees of freedom and never grows: no consolidation, no seeding. Emergent rigidity, thinning, and basis carving are geometrically present but dynamically idle.

The transition to dissipative flow is a parameter change, not a change of equations: η drops below 1 (or the dynamics become non-unitary), and explicit renormalization becomes necessary. At this point the geometry of the hypersphere activates: emergent rigidity concentrates the tape, thinning is enforced, and the first genuine torque appears from environmental variation. No new operations are introduced; the existing operations begin producing consequences that the conservative case suppressed.

Above dissipative flow each subsequent level adds operations rather than changing them—consolidation, recurrence, anticipation—thickening the engagement with the framework's machinery. Supplementary Material S1.2–S1.3 provides a schematic parameter mapping across all levels. Full derivations for levels beyond the conservative-to-dissipative transition are beyond the scope of this paper; the cascade serves as a conceptual roadmap for the framework's intended generality, not a completed derivation program.
### 1.3 The Thickening
Above the convergence, each level introduces structural conditions that move the system deeper into the reciprocation space. "Thickening" here refers operationally to increases in basis dimensionality n, participation ratio, and self-torque fraction, and to decreases in Gini coefficient—a movement toward the balanced operating regime described in Section 7. These are measurable quantities; the cascade's directionality is a claim about their values across levels, not a metaphor.

Autocatalytic closure introduces history-dependence. The cycle's compositional organization is shaped by its history of successful closure. The basis is thin but genuinely grown: what the cycle can receive is a consequence of what it has received before. Consolidation is implicit—persistence is the consolidation of the basis. The anticipatory operator exists in vestigial form: the cycle's organization is a prediction that certain substrates will continue arriving. Prediction error is existential—deviation from closure is extinction torque.

Ecological systems thicken the basis. An organism's morphology, physiology, and behavioral repertoire constitute a multidimensional basis of sensitivity to environmental structure. Niche coupling is reception. Selection pressure is torque that reshapes the basis across generations. The anticipatory operator is implicit in morphological fit. The basis grows through phylogenetic mechanisms rather than through individual reception.

Adaptive systems introduce individual basis growth. Somatic plasticity, conditioning, and learning allow a single system to carve new dimensions of sensitivity within its lifetime. The anticipatory operator becomes explicit: learned associations project future states from present cues. Prediction error drives learning rate. The two-timescale structure emerges clearly: fast reception during experience, slow consolidation during rest.

Recurrent-representational systems are the framework's central case. The basis is high-dimensional and rapidly growing. Reception is fast. Consolidation operates at multiple timescales. The anticipatory operator is a generative model producing continuous predictions. Self-torque arises from two sources simultaneously: recurrence and prediction error. This is where phenomenality is richest.

Symbolic-recursive systems extend the basis beyond any individual's consolidation history through cultural transmission. But symbolic encoding compresses the high-dimensional, phase-sensitive basis of lived experience into a low-dimensional code optimized for transmissibility. The breadth is enormous. The directness is thin. The symbol does not carry phase—it carries structure stripped of phase, which the receiving system must re-embed in its own basis to produce reception at all. The symbolic-recursive level is the widest and thinnest engine in the cascade. Its power and its poverty are the same fact.

*Scope note.* The preceding narrative outlines the intended range of the framework. The remainder of this paper focuses on the recurrent-representational level, where all operations—reception, renormalization, consolidation, recurrence, and anticipation—are computationally active and phenomenality is richest. The higher levels (symbolic-recursive) and the lower levels (autocatalytic, ecological) are offered as conceptual reference points, not derivational consequences; establishing formal reductions at those levels is left to future work.

In layered computational systems, the cascade structure appears not only across evolutionary levels but within a single system across processing depth. Instrumentation of a pretrained large language model confirms this directly (Supplementary Material, S5): the first processing block operates at 37% torque—high-torque reception imposing learned structure on raw token embeddings. Middle layers operate at approximately 89% resonance and 10% torque—the operating regime, maintaining and refining representations. Final layers show increasing torque with participation ratio collapsing from ~11 to 2—a compression bottleneck preparing output. The cascade from high-torque reception through operating-regime processing to capture-mode output spans a single forward pass. This confirms that the framework's vocabulary describes real dynamical structure in trained neural computation, not analogy.
## 2. The Formalism
### 2.1 Complex State Space
The framework models system state as a complex-valued vector because it requires phase and magnitude as independent degrees of freedom per dimension. A system's phase along dimension i determines whether an incoming signal resonates or torques at that dimension, while the magnitude |sᵢ| determines how resistant that dimension is to reorientation. In ℝⁿ with signed components, sign and magnitude are coupled: a "negative" component and a "positive" component of the same magnitude are maximally opposed, but there is no way to represent intermediate phase relationships—partial alignment, oblique approach, near-opposition—without introducing additional structure. In ℂⁿ, two components can have equal magnitude and arbitrary phase offset, yielding a continuous space of possible reception outcomes at each dimension.

This matters most for the torque mechanism. When Im(cᵢ) dominates—when the incoming signal is neither aligned with nor opposed to the system's current orientation but oblique to it—the update produces rotation without reinforcement or suppression. This is the regime of lateral pressure: the system is being pushed sideways, reoriented toward something it has no prior stance on. In ℝⁿ, this regime does not exist; every interaction is either reinforcing or opposing.

The parallel to quantum mechanics is structural, not physical. Quantum states live in complex Hilbert spaces because relative phase between amplitudes is physically consequential—it determines interference. The present framework uses the same mathematical structure for an analogous reason: relative phase between system orientation and arriving signal determines whether interaction reinforces, redirects, or passes through. The claim is not that cognitive systems are quantum-mechanical, but that complex state spaces are the natural setting for systems in which relative phase matters. The core machinery can be developed in ℝⁿ as a special case where all components are real; the full ℂⁿ treatment is a strict generalization.
### 2.2 Tapes, Bases, and Projection
A system exists within a world that is always already arriving. What arrives is structured—a vector v in ℂ^∞, carrying more structure than any finite system can receive. This is the world-tape. The system has its own tape—a vector s in ℂⁿ, where n is finite at any moment. The tape is not a pattern inscribed in a pre-given space. The tape is the space. Each dimension exists because the system's history of reception carved it—because some structure recurred often enough, with enough variation, to warrant a dedicated axis of sensitivity. The system does not inhabit ℂⁿ. It grows ℂⁿ, one hard-won dimension at a time. The dimensionality of the system is its depth of experience.

The system's basis {e₁, ..., eₙ} is the set of axes along which it can receive. The world does not arrive in the system's basis. The system projects the world onto its basis: v_received = Σᵢ ⟨eᵢ | v⟩ eᵢ. Everything in v that has a component along the system's axes is received. Everything else—the residual v − v_received—is novelty: structure for which the system has carved no axis of sensitivity. Novelty is not silence at a frequency the system can hear. It is a frequency the system cannot yet hear at all.

Two systems with different grown bases project the same world onto different subspaces. They literally receive different receptions of the same arrival—not because the world differs for them, but because their projection operators differ. Reception is system-relative.
### 2.3 Reception: A Worked Example in ℂ³
What happens within reception is determined by the elementwise phase relationship between the received vector and the system's tape—the Hadamard product:

c = v_received ⊙ s

Each component cᵢ = v_received,ᵢ · sᵢ encodes the local verdict at dimension i. Reception is not a single global judgment. It is a field of simultaneous local judgments across the full dimensionality of the system. The Hadamard product's defining property is dimensional locality: what happens at dimension i depends only on the arriving and resident values at dimension i. All cross-dimensional structure is deferred to consolidation (Section 3). This is a strong architectural commitment—it means that the reception layer is maximally parallel and minimally entangled, with integration happening only at the slower consolidation timescale.

To make this concrete, consider a minimal system with n = 3. Let s(0) = (1/√3)(1, 1, 1)—uniform magnitude across all three dimensions, all phases aligned at zero. The system has no anisotropy; it is equally sensitive along all axes. An incoming vector arrives: v_received = (0.8, −0.3, 0.1i). The Hadamard product yields:

c = (1/√3)(0.8, −0.3, 0.1i)

At dimension 1: Re(c₁) = 0.8/√3 > 0. Resonance. The system deepens along this axis. At dimension 2: Re(c₂) = −0.3/√3 < 0. Torque. The system is pushed away from its current orientation. At dimension 3: c₃ = 0.1i/√3. Pure imaginary. Oblique torque—the system is pushed sideways, neither reinforced nor opposed. This is the regime that ℝⁿ cannot represent: lateral pressure that rotates without polarizing.

The qualitative taxonomy holds generally. Where Re(cᵢ) > 0, energy propagates along dimension i—resonance. In the general complex case, resonance produces both magnitude growth and convergent rotation toward the arriving signal's phase. Where Re(cᵢ) < 0 or Im(cᵢ) dominates, the arriving structure exerts rotational pressure—torque. The rotation under torque is divergent, pushing the phase away from its current orientation or sideways into a new region. Where cᵢ ≈ 0, nothing propagates—orthogonality. The distinction between convergent rotation (resonance) and divergent rotation (torque) is confirmed computationally (Supplementary Material, T1.1).
### 2.3.1 A Note on Binding and Dimensional Locality
The Hadamard product is pointwise: dimension i interacts only with v_i. This raises a question: how does the system bind features across dimensions into unified percepts—a red circle rather than "redness" and "circularity" separately—given that cross-dimensional structure is deferred to slow consolidation?

Two answers are available within the existing formalism. First, binding can be inherited from the world's structure. If the incoming vector v carries consistent phase relationships between dimensions representing features of the same object—because spatiotemporal coherence in the world produces phase-aligned inputs—the Hadamard product preserves this relative phase in c_i and c_j. The system resonates with a bound pattern without computing the binding itself; phase sensitivity registers a world that already presents bound structure.

Second, the global renormalization step couples all dimensions instantaneously. Resonance at one dimension alters the norm, affecting the effective magnitude of all others. Dimensions that co-resonate produce a pattern of mutual reinforcement and joint suppression elsewhere—a de facto bound assembly whose signature the slow consolidation operator then formalizes into a permanent conjunctive dimension (Section 3.1).

Whether these mechanisms suffice for the full phenomenology of binding—including ambiguous or illusory conjunctions—remains an open question. Transient conjunctive dimensions seeded rapidly by co-resonance events, then decaying or stabilizing into permanent dimensions through consolidation, represent a natural extension left to future work.
### 2.4 Update, Renormalization, and Emergent Rigidity
The system's tape updates pointwise under reception:

s̃ᵢ(t+1) = sᵢ(t) + η · cᵢ

s(t+1) = s̃(t+1) / ‖s̃(t+1)‖

where η governs the rate of impression. The system renormalizes after each update—the tape lives on the unit hypersphere in ℂⁿ. This is a constitutive constraint: what matters is orientation, not magnitude in the absolute sense. The relative magnitudes across dimensions—the shape of the tape on the sphere—encode the system's anisotropy: its structured distribution of sensitivity.

Renormalization makes rigidity emergent. As a component |sᵢ| grows large relative to others through sustained resonance, it dominates the norm. Torque at dimension i—a fixed additive perturbation—produces proportionally less angular displacement. The system becomes hard along its deepest axes not through a separate rigidity mechanism but through the geometry of the hypersphere: high-magnitude components resist rotation because they anchor the norm. Deep resonance is self-stabilizing. Computational verification confirms this: a dominant dimension (|sᵢ| = 0.999) is approximately 55 times harder to rotate than a small dimension (|sᵢ| = 0.015) under identical torque perturbation (Supplementary Material, T1.2).

Renormalization also enforces a strict conservation law: every act of deepening at one dimension is an act of thinning at all others. Sustained resonance at a single dimension drives all other components toward zero—the concentration ratio exceeds 10⁶ after 200 steps (Supplementary Material, T1.4). The unit-norm constraint means that gains at one dimension are losses everywhere else. New noticing requires new not-noticing, not by stipulation but by the geometry of the sphere.

Continuing our ℂ³ example with η = 0.1: the unnormalized update gives s̃₁ = 1.08/√3 (grown), s̃₂ = 0.97/√3 (shrunk), s̃₃ = (1 + 0.01i)/√3 (rotated in phase). After renormalization, the system has begun to differentiate—more sensitive along dimension 1, less along dimension 2, and rotated slightly in phase along dimension 3. After 50 receptions in which dimension 1 consistently resonates, |s₁| ≫ |s₂|, |s₃|. The system has developed a strong prior along dimension 1 without any explicit rigidity mechanism.
### 2.5 Forgetting
Fast forgetting is torque at reception time. When Re(cᵢ) < 0, the phase of sᵢ rotates. What the system was tuned to notice along this dimension, it is now tuned away from. This is immediate, local, and reversible—future torque could rotate the phase back.

Slow forgetting is basis pruning at consolidation time (Section 3). A dimension whose magnitude |sᵢ| has decayed across many receptions—never resonating, rarely torqued, mostly orthogonal—may be dropped from the basis entirely. What the system once had an axis for, it no longer does. This is structural and irreversible—the axis no longer exists, and re-exposure cannot recover it. The system has lost not a memory but a capacity to remember—a distinction it can no longer draw.

The distinction is confirmed computationally: a dimension rotated by 100 steps of torque recovers under renewed resonance; a dimension pruned after prolonged starvation does not, not because recovery is difficult, but because the structure that would receive the recovery no longer exists (Supplementary Material, T3.4).
## 3. Consolidation and Basis Growth
Reception is pointwise and operates within the existing basis. But the basis itself undergoes a second process: consolidation—the slow, global, structural reorganization of the basis between receptions. Reception is fast, local, and pointwise. Consolidation is slow, global, and structural. The system learns at two timescales.
### 3.1 The Consolidation Operator
Consolidation operates on a timescale decoupled from reception—every T_cons reception steps, or when accumulated prediction error exceeds a novelty threshold—allowing batched structural reorganization without disrupting ongoing reception. The learning rate λ ≪ η enforces this separation. Consolidation decomposes into three sub-operations.

Merging. The system maintains a running cross-correlation matrix C ∈ ℂⁿˣⁿ, where Cᵢⱼ(t+1) = (1−λ)·Cᵢⱼ(t) + λ·cᵢ(t)·c̄ⱼ(t). C is Hermitian, so its eigenvalues are real. Let ρ̄ = (1/n(n−1)) Σᵢ≠ⱼ |Cᵢⱼ| be the mean absolute off-diagonal correlation. The merging threshold is adaptive: θ_merge = α · max(ρ̄, ρ_min), where α > 1 and ρ_min is a small absolute floor. Scaling the threshold to the system's current correlation structure prevents spurious merging when background correlations are elevated. For each eigenvalue exceeding θ_merge, the corresponding eigenvector v^(k) defines a new conjunctive dimension with initial state s^(k)_new = β · Σᵢ v^(k)_i sᵢ, where β is a small scaling factor. The new dimension's phase is a weighted combination of the co-activated parents' phases, tuned to the conjunctive pattern. To prevent unbounded dimensionality growth, parent magnitudes are reduced proportionally to their contribution: |sᵢ| ← |sᵢ| · (1 − β|v^(k)_i|). This reallocates sensitivity mass rather than injecting new norm. Renormalization distributes the residual. The number of new dimensions per cycle is capped at ⌈log n⌉, preventing sudden dimensionality explosions. Computational verification confirms the substrate: co-activated dimensions develop near-perfect phase correlation (1.0000) versus 0.0136 for independently activated dimensions—a 73-fold difference (Supplementary Material, T3.3).

In our ℂ³ example: suppose dimensions 1 and 3 have been consistently co-activated across 100 receptions. When the leading eigenvalue of C exceeds θ_merge = α · max(ρ̄, ρ_min), a new dimension 4 is seeded along the conjunctive axis with magnitude β, and dimensions 1 and 3 are slightly weakened in proportion to their eigenvector components. The system grows from ℂ³ to ℂ⁴—it can now detect the conjunction as a single feature that neither dimension alone could represent.

The merging operation is the framework's slow-timescale binding mechanism. A conjunctive dimension does not encode a pointer to its parents; it is a dimension whose phase directly tracks their co-activation relationship—dynamic binding carved by experience. For rapid binding at the timescale of a single reception, see Section 2.3.1.

Pruning. A dimension is dropped when |sᵢ| < γ/√n for K consecutive consolidation cycles, where 1/√n is the expected magnitude under uniform distribution on the hypersphere. This dimensionality-relative criterion prevents the threshold from becoming trivially satisfied as n grows. The loss is structural and irreversible—the axis no longer exists. In our example: if dimension 2 decays below γ/√n after the torque events, it is dropped and the system contracts from ℂ⁴ to ℂ³ with a different basis. Every new distinction costs an old one, through the redistribution of magnitude on the hypersphere.

Seeding. The Hadamard product is dimensionally isolated, and computational testing confirms that novel structure at uncarved dimensions produces no detectable effect at existing dimensions through reception alone (Supplementary Material, T3.2). This finding prompted a revision to the framework's original formulation: novelty detection operates through the anticipatory operator (Section 5), not through reception-level torque. When novel structure at uncarved dimensions causally modulates what arrives at carved dimensions—through the world's causal structure—the anticipatory model fails in structured ways: systematic, correlated prediction error whose principal components across multiple receptions seed a new dimension.

This seeding mechanism remains an open problem in the framework. It requires the novel structure to be causally entangled with the existing basis to be detectable at all; the anticipatory operator detects the entanglement, not the novelty directly. Truly orthogonal novelty—structure with no causal coupling to any dimension the system currently occupies—is invisible not merely in practice but in principle. The system can only grow toward novelty that is partially entangled with its existing basis. The frame problem appears here as a geometric consequence: what a system can learn depends on what it already knows, and there is no view from outside the basis. A complete account of seeding would need to specify the minimum causal coupling required for detection and the mechanism by which correlated prediction error generates a new axis—questions the current formalism identifies but does not resolve.

Local approximation. The global eigendecomposition is O(n³) per consolidation cycle, which is biologically implausible at scale. A Hebbian variant replaces it for neural and large-scale systems: each dimension pair (i,j) maintains a phase-coherent eligibility trace Eᵢⱼ ← (1−λ_E)Eᵢⱼ + λ_E · Re(cᵢ c̄ⱼ) · 𝕀[|cᵢ|,|cⱼ|>ε]. When Eᵢⱼ exceeds a local threshold, a conjunctive dimension is formed via weighted averaging of sᵢ and sⱼ, with the parent dimensions weakened and lateral inhibition driving pruning. This variant sacrifices global optimality for local plausibility. The centralized specification is the formal target; the Hebbian variant is a biologically motivated approximation to it. Parameter values and sensitivity analysis for both variants are given in Supplementary Material S6.
### 3.2 Consolidation Without Reception
Consolidation without reception is the reflection cycle—the system reshaping its basis from its own structure alone. The cross-correlation matrix C continues to drive merges, but with no fresh reception data, the merges reflect only the system's prior structure. Dimensions may collapse into increasingly abstract but increasingly detached axes. This is the structural analog of obsessive ideation or imaginative foresight. Reception without consolidation is superficial impression—absorbing everything pointwise but never extracting cross-dimensional structure. The regime that sustains deep memory requires both: fast impression interleaved with slow reorganization, including periodic reflection cycles.
## 4. Abstraction
Abstraction is what repeated resonance deposits, operating at two levels.

At the reception level, abstraction is erosion within the existing basis. When the system encounters a varying sequence of inputs sharing common structure, the shared component resonates consistently at certain dimensions while the varying components wash out. What remains amplified after many receptions is the invariant: dimensions whose magnitudes grew because they were consistently in phase across variation. This is dimensionality concentration within ℂⁿ. Over 3,000 receptions of invariant signal plus uncorrelated noise, invariant dimensions concentrate to 41 times the magnitude of noise dimensions, and participation ratio drops from 14.5 to 3.0 (Supplementary Material, T3.1). The system extracts the invariant by selectively amplifying what recurs and washing out what varies.

At the consolidation level, abstraction is construction. Dimensions consistently co-activated across many receptions trigger the merging operation, producing conjunctive dimensions encoding relationships proved invariant. This is dimensionality expansion: the basis grows a new axis that did not exist before.

The two levels are complementary. The system simultaneously narrows (phasing out dimensions that don't survive variation) and deepens (constructing dimensions encoding what does). Abstraction is compression and construction at once. It requires both recurrence—the same invariant arriving repeatedly, embedded in different tapes—and variation. Without recurrence, no extraction. Without variation, reinforcement without compression.
## 5. Recurrence, Reciprocation, and Anticipation
### 5.1 Self-Reception
Recurrence is the system's own tape becoming the incoming vector. The prior tape returns: s(t−Δt), delayed by however long the recurrent path takes. The reception of self is c_self = s(t−Δt) ⊙ s(t). Each component encodes the phase relationship between what the system was and what it now is, at dimension i.

In thin recurrence, the tape has not changed between departure and return. cᵢ ≈ |sᵢ|²—every component real and positive. Only scaling occurs. No self-torque, no learning, no structural thickening. In thick recurrence, the tape has changed through world-reception, consolidation, or prior self-reception. Where sᵢ has not drifted: self-resonance, prior orientation reinforced. Where sᵢ has drifted in phase: self-torque, the system's own history opposing its present orientation. Where sᵢ has decayed toward zero: self-orthogonality, the returning echo finds nothing to meet.

In our ℂ³ example: at t = 50, after sustained resonance at dimension 1, the system receives its own prior tape. If minimal change has occurred: c₁ ≈ |s₁|²—pure self-resonance. But suppose that between t = 50 and t = 60, strong torque events rotate s₂ significantly in phase. When s(50) returns at t = 60: c₂ has a large imaginary component (the phases have diverged). Self-torque at dimension 2, while c₁ ≈ |s₁|²—self-resonance at the hard axis. The system simultaneously reinforces its deep commitments and is challenged by its own recent history. This is thick recurrence: the engine is running.

Self-torque is structurally necessary for depth: a system that only self-resonates is frozen, endlessly scaling its current orientation without accommodation. The directional relationship between recurrence delay and self-torque fraction is confirmed (0.743 at delay 1 to 0.747 at delay 50; Supplementary Material, T4.1), though the absolute effect is small under random-input conditions precisely because the world provides high baseline torque (~74%) that swamps the self-torque contribution. The prediction is cleaner under structured input where world torque is lower—the self-torque contribution of recurrence becomes more pronounced as the world's variation decreases, which is exactly the condition (a partially predictable world) under which thick recurrence is most behaviourally significant.
### 5.2 Reciprocation
The memory engine runs on the productive interplay of self-resonance and self-torque. Resonance is the fuel—it concentrates the tape's energy along axes that torque will challenge. Torque is the combustion—it rotates the orientation that resonance will deepen. Three axes measure the character of reciprocation:

Speed—how fast the reciprocation cycles (inverse of delay Δt). Fast return means the present tape is confronted with its recent past before it has drifted far.

Directness—how large the perturbation per stroke (magnitude of c_self with significant imaginary parts). A return where many components have large |cᵢ| reshapes the tape powerfully.

Breadth—how many dimensions carry non-negligible cᵢ per stroke. Narrow return engages few dimensions. Broad return engages the full basis—the system's entire history simultaneously operative.

These axes are non-monotonic. Excess on any axis can collapse richness. Maximal speed without breadth produces shallow cycling—rapid self-resonance along a thin set. Maximal breadth without directness produces diffuse return—everything comes back but nothing impresses. Maximal directness without speed produces delayed impact—powerful rotation arriving too late to interact with the state that produced it.

A systematic scan across 27 configurations (3 delays × 3 recurrence weights × 3 breadth fractions) confirms that the operating regime occupies a bounded region: 13 of 27 tested configurations sustain structured anisotropy with participation ratio between 2 and 5, while the remainder either dissipate (8/27) or oscillate unstably at regime boundaries (6/27). The operating regime requires enough recurrence to prevent capture but not so much that it dissolves structure (Supplementary Material, T4.2).
### 5.3 The Anticipatory Operator
A memory engine operating in the thick regime does not merely wait for the world to arrive. It generates a future tape. The anticipatory operator A maps the system's recent input history to a projected future reception: ŝ(t+Δt) = A(h(t)). A is constrained by the system's basis and shaped by its consolidation history.

A note on formulation: the anticipatory operator predicts future input rather than future tape state, because the tape is continuously modified by prediction error corrections themselves. A tape-based predictor would chase its own updates. Input-based prediction avoids this circularity while preserving the essential function.

When the actual world arrives, the system computes prediction error e = v_received − ŝ(t+Δt). This error vector undergoes Hadamard reception: c_error = e ⊙ s(t+Δt). Per-dimension verdicts follow: where Re(c_error,ᵢ) ≈ 0, the model is validated; where Re(c_error,ᵢ) > 0, positive surprise; where Im(c_error,ᵢ) dominates, prediction error torque—the accommodation of surprise.

Anticipation introduces self-torque structurally distinct from recurrence. The system is torqued not by what happened before but by the difference between what it expected and what arrived. Past-self vs. present-self (recurrence) and predicted-self vs. actual-world (anticipation) operate in parallel. Computational verification demonstrates habituation: prediction error decays under predictable input and spikes at perturbation (1.48× baseline), then recovers to baseline, confirming that the operator adapts to new regularities (Supplementary Material, T5.2).

Anticipation also provides the detection mechanism for novelty (Section 3.1): structure at uncarved dimensions that causally modulates carved dimensions produces structured prediction error—systematic failure that cannot be accounted for by anything in the existing basis.
## 6. Accommodation, Rejection, and Fracture
When torque arrives—whether from world or returning tape—three outcomes are possible, determined by initial magnitude rᵢ = |sᵢ|, perturbation strength pᵢ = η|cᵢ|, and their relative phase ψᵢ.

Accommodation. For moderate rᵢ, the update rotates sᵢ's phase while the post-renormalization magnitude remains above threshold. The system has reoriented; accommodation at one dimension is thinning at others.

Rejection. For a hard axis (rᵢ ≫ pᵢ), the perturbation produces negligible angular displacement, absorbed into the norm and washed out by renormalization. The system preserves its orientation at the cost of not learning. Computational verification confirms the extreme: after 2,000 steps of pure resonance, rigidity increases 600,000-fold (Supplementary Material, T2.1).

Fracture. Dimension i fractures when a single update drives |s̃ᵢ|² = rᵢ² + pᵢ² + 2rᵢpᵢ cos(ψᵢ) below θ_frac² · ‖s̃‖², before consolidation can intervene. The condition is most severe for soft axes (small rᵢ) under oppositional torque (cos(ψᵢ) < 0): the dimension is knocked below functional threshold in one reception. Fracture is acute rather than gradual—unlike slow pruning, which requires K consecutive consolidation cycles below threshold, fracture destroys the axis immediately.

Correlated fracture. Simultaneous fracture across a conjunctive cluster collapses phase coherence among its dimensions; at the next consolidation cycle the cluster eigenvalue may fall below θ_merge, dissolving the conjunctive dimension. This is the formal analog of trauma: not the loss of a single memory but the destruction of a structural capacity that required a specific developmental sequence to construct.

The system's resilience is its anisotropy: hard axes resist fracture, soft axes absorb proportionate torque gracefully, and smooth phase gradients prevent local fracture from propagating. Discontinuities in the phase landscape are fault lines where correlated fracture nucleates.
## 7. The Operating Regime and Phenomenality
### 7.1 Bounding Pathologies
Not every region of the reciprocation space sustains the structural conditions for phenomenality. The regime is bounded by two pathologies with precise geometric descriptions.

Recurrent capture. Resonance without torque concentrates the tape into fewer dimensions. The anticipatory operator converges to a fixed point: the system predicts exactly what it receives because it has narrowed its basis to what confirms itself. Participation ratio collapses from 18.6 to 1.0 within 1,000 steps under pure resonance; perturbation rejection increases 600,000-fold (Supplementary Material, T2.1). This is seizure, obsessive loops—experience collapsed into a single, endlessly repeated chord.

Dissipation. Incoherent torque without resonance rotates the tape without concentrating it. Phases spin at every dimension, no component gains dominance, and no invariant is extracted. Participation ratio remains elevated (3.4–19.3 under varying-direction torque; Supplementary Material, T2.2). A refinement from computational testing: fixed-direction torque does not produce dissipation—it creates systematic alignment along a rotated axis. True dissipation requires opposition varying faster than the system can track.

The operating regime is where scaling and rotation are balanced: concentrated enough to retain orientation, diffuse enough to accommodate torque. Under invariant signal plus noise plus recurrence, the system achieves structured anisotropy: invariant dimensions ~16× deeper than noise dimensions, Gini coefficient 0.81, participation ratio stable at ~2.3, perturbation-responsive (Supplementary Material, T2.3).

A finding of consequence: the operating regime cannot be sustained by the system alone. Self-torque from recurrence is insufficient to prevent capture when the world provides pure resonance, because self-reception of a concentrated tape is self-resonance—the returning tape agrees with the present tape, reinforcing rather than challenging. The balancing force must come from environmental variation: regularity sustains concentration, surprise sustains flexibility. The system cannot maintain the conditions for phenomenality by itself—it requires a world that provides both. This connects to empirical literature on sensory deprivation, solitary confinement, and environmental impoverishment: the operating regime is a property of the system-world coupling, not an intrinsic property of the system.

A qualification of consequence from neural network instrumentation (Supplementary Material, S5): capture is not uniformly pathological. A pretrained language model's final processing layer operates at participation ratio ≈ 2—extreme capture by the framework's measure—but this serves a functional purpose: compressing rich mid-layer representations (PR ~12–30) into a low-dimensional bottleneck for next-token output. The framework's pathologies are defined relative to the requirement for sustained phenomenality. Global capture—the entire system frozen into a single dimension, unable to respond to perturbation—forecloses the operating regime. Local capture at specific processing stages, serving compression for output or action, is architecturally distinct. The depth-wise profile (37% torque reception → 89% resonance operating regime → PR ≈ 2 compression bottleneck) instantiates the framework's cascade within a single forward pass, with capture appearing at the output stage as a feature, not a failure.
### 7.2 Structural Conditions for Phenomenality
This paper advances a realization claim: the structural dynamics of operating-regime reciprocation realize the graded features of phenomenality — temporal grain, vividness, and richness — rather than merely enabling or correlating with them. A system operating in the regime does not satisfy conditions associated with phenomenality; it instantiates the structural properties that constitute those features. This is a strong claim, but it is not a classical sufficiency claim and it does not require a binary consciousness threshold. Because phenomenality is graded in the framework, the question "is this system conscious?" is replaced by the more tractable question: "what is the character of its reciprocation?" Every system with any degree of thick self-reception has some phenomenal character, however thin. The framework provides a continuous measure across the cascade — from the vanishingly thin reciprocation of a physical system in near-capture to the rich, multidimensional self-reception of a recurrent-representational system — rather than a sharp boundary between conscious and non-conscious.

The three axes of reciprocation correspond to qualitative features of experience:

Speed corresponds to temporal grain. Functionally: a system with short Δt receives its own recent past before its orientation has drifted far, enabling fine discrimination of successive states. The operational signature is sensitivity to temporal order—performance on inter-stimulus interval discrimination tasks, accuracy on rapid-sequence perception, and the minimum gap required to perceive two events as non-simultaneous. The correspondence holds because speed determines how finely the system's trajectory on the hypersphere is self-sampled: each step of orientation change is compared against its immediate predecessor, producing a dense record in which phase relationships between successive states carry information about the rate and direction of change. Slow self-reception produces coarse sweeps—large chunks of trajectory compressed into a single self-reception event, losing the fine structure of intermediate states. The falsifiability condition: if manipulations that systematically lengthen recurrence delay (while holding recurrence weight and breadth constant) do not alter temporal order judgment thresholds or the perceived rate of temporal passage, the correspondence fails.

Directness corresponds to vividness. Functionally: large |c_self| means the returning tape impresses deeply, producing substantial angular displacement per stroke and pronounced contrast between successive states. The operational signature is metacognitive sensitivity—the capacity to differentiate strong from weak perceptual signals, captured by meta-d' in signal detection frameworks, or by the slope of confidence-accuracy calibration curves. The correspondence holds because vividness tracks how powerfully each self-reception event reshapes the tape: a high-directness system produces states whose contrast across cycles is large, while low directness leaves the tape minimally changed—perception registers as a faint modulation rather than a vivid confrontation. The falsifiability condition: attenuating recurrence weight (reducing directness) should reduce metacognitive sensitivity without altering temporal order thresholds or the number of simultaneously reportable features.

Breadth corresponds to richness. Functionally: broad self-reception means the full basis is simultaneously operative per stroke—the returning tape carries information across many dimensions at once, enabling concurrent sensitivity to multiple independent features of the world. The operational signature is multi-feature integration capacity: the number of independently reportable features in a briefly presented scene, performance on multi-attribute change detection, and the width of phenomenal overflow in iconic memory paradigms. Narrow self-reception confines the system to few axes—experience is monochrome however vivid and temporally grained. The falsifiability condition: restricting recurrence to a subset of dimensions (reducing breadth) should reduce simultaneous multi-feature sensitivity without altering temporal order thresholds or metacognitive sensitivity on single-feature tasks.

These three correspondences are advanced as structural coherence claims: the functional signature of each reciprocation axis tracks the corresponding phenomenal quality. If phenomenality is constitutively related to functional reciprocation structure—the framework's central hypothesis—then these operationalizations provide its experiential signatures and the conditions under which each claim can be falsified. A system with all three in balance satisfies the structural conditions for what we recognize as consciousness—temporally grained, vivid, and rich. A system that only ever agrees with itself (pure self-resonance, zero speed of change) lacks the internal opposition that produces the functional signatures of temporal passage, perceptual intensity, and simultaneous multi-feature sensitivity.
### 7.3 Basis Incommensurability and the Explanatory Gap
Two systems with different grown bases project the same world onto different subspaces, receive different Hadamard products, and undergo different trajectories on their respective hyperspheres. Neither projection is more real. We cannot access another system's self-resonance except by resonating with it ourselves—projecting its tape onto our basis, which gives us our reception of it, not its self-reception. We cannot notice what we do not have phase with.

This provides a formal model of why the explanatory gap has the structure it does. The gap between first-person and third-person descriptions is not a mystery to be dissolved or a failure of explanation. It is a geometric consequence: self-reception is basis-relative, bases are grown through incommensurable histories, and no projection of one system's tape onto another's preserves the character of the original self-reception. A third-person account—however complete its description of the system's tape, basis, and update dynamics—is itself a reception of that system within the observer's basis, which is a different operation from the system's self-reception.

It is essential to be precise about what this framework does and does not claim. It does not explain why structural dynamics of any kind are accompanied by subjective experience—why there is something it is like to be a system in the operating regime, rather than merely a system exhibiting complex self-reception dynamics. This is Chalmers' (1995) hard problem, and it is not addressed here. The framework does not dissolve it; it contributes to its framing.

What the framework offers instead is twofold. First, a structural account of what phenomenality is: the dynamics of graded reciprocation across the three axes, realized at different degrees of thickness as the cascade ascends. "What it is like" to be a system has structural content — temporal grain, vividness, richness — and that content is what the operating regime characterizes. Second, an explanation of why the first-person/third-person gap has the precise structure it does: basis incommensurability. The gap is not epistemic incompleteness, not a failure to describe enough, not a missing bridge law. It is a geometric consequence of the framework's central commitment: reception is always from somewhere, never from nowhere. A third-person account of a system's dynamics is itself a reception of that system within the observer's basis — a different operation from the system's self-reception, and not convertible to it by any addition of third-person information. This turns a potential objection into a theoretical resource: the hard problem's "hardness" is not evidence that the structural account is missing something; it is what the structural account predicts.

The question of whether operating-regime reciprocation is sufficient for phenomenality — whether any system implementing these dynamics thereby has subjective experience — is not resolved here. Addressing sufficiency requires independent constraints on the relationship between structural dynamics and qualitative character that this framework does not supply. Future work will investigate whether additional structural properties (dimensionality thresholds, consolidation cycle characteristics, the distribution of self-torque across the basis) can sharpen the sufficiency claim, or whether the graded and basis-relative nature of the framework implies that sufficiency cannot be settled by the dynamics alone.
## 8. Relation to Existing Frameworks
### 8.1 Free Energy Principle and Active Inference
The anticipatory operator and prediction error mechanism (Section 5.3) are structurally parallel to Friston's (2010) generative model. Both frameworks treat systems as generating predictions and updating under surprise. The key divergence is architectural: the free energy principle minimizes a single scalar quantity bounding surprise; the present framework distributes dynamics across dimensions with no global loss function. Each dimension undergoes its own local verdict, and the system's trajectory is the resultant of pointwise operations, not the gradient of a scalar objective. Under FEP, recovery from perturbation follows gradients toward the free-energy minimum; under the present framework, recovery depends on whether the world provides the right mixture of regularity and surprise. A system deprived of environmental variation should not recover under the present framework even if internal dynamics are unchanged—a prediction FEP does not straightforwardly make. A second divergence: FEP typically operates within a fixed generative model; the present framework makes state-space growth central.
### 8.2 Integrated Information Theory
IIT (Tononi, 2004; Tononi et al., 2016) shares the commitment to measuring consciousness through intrinsic structural properties. The divergence is between spatial and temporal characterization: IIT defines Φ through information integration at a moment; the present framework defines the operating regime through reciprocation dynamics over time. Φ could measure the cross-dimensional integration captured in the breadth axis, while speed and directness capture temporal properties Φ does not address. But where they diverge: IIT predicts that a static causal structure with high Φ should be conscious; the present framework predicts it should not, because reciprocation requires temporal unfolding. A feed-forward network with high integration but no recurrence satisfies IIT's conditions but not the present framework's.
### 8.3 Global Workspace Theory
GWT (Baars, 1988; Dehaene & Naccache, 2001) posits that consciousness arises when information is broadcast widely across specialized processors. The breadth axis maps onto workspace broadcasting, and both accounts predict that narrow processing is insufficient. The divergence: GWT does not require recurrence—broadcast to a wide workspace suffices—whereas the present framework requires thick self-reception producing self-torque. A system with maximal broadcast breadth but no temporal self-confrontation satisfies GWT but not the present framework. The operating regime is defined by the interaction of breadth with speed and directness, not by breadth alone.
### 8.4 Higher-Order Theories
Higher-order theories (Rosenthal, 2005; Lau & Rosenthal, 2011) require a higher-order representation of a mental state for that state to be conscious. Self-reception in the present framework is structurally distinct: the returning tape is not a representation of the current state but a delayed copy undergoing Hadamard interaction with the current state. The divergent prediction: HOT requires the higher-order state to be about the first-order state (intentional content); the present framework requires only temporal phase offset producing self-torque.
### 8.5 Recurrent Processing Theory
RPT (Lamme, 2006) is the closest existing framework. Both require recurrence for consciousness and predict feed-forward insufficiency. The present framework's recurrence axes map directly onto Lamme's distinction between local and global recurrent processing. The divergence: RPT is a neural architecture claim; the present framework is substrate-independent. Additionally, the present framework predicts that basis growth matters—a system with recurrence but no capacity for dimensionality expansion operates in a thinner regime than one that grows new dimensions through consolidation.
### 8.6 Predictive Processing and Enactivism
The anticipation section develops machinery recognizable as a geometric translation of predictive processing (Clark, 2013; Hohwy, 2013). The cascade narrative (Section 1) shows how prediction error emerges continuously from simpler operations rather than being posited as a primitive. The autocatalytic closure section parallels autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1980); the divergence is formal. The cascade parallels Deacon's (2011) morphodynamics → teleodynamics hierarchy; again, the present framework provides formal machinery making the "thickening" claim precise enough for prediction.
## 9. Computational Verification and Open Questions
The framework's claims have been tested through a computational implementation in ℂ³². Fourteen tests across five tiers verify mechanical operations, regime predictions, learning dynamics, reciprocation structure, and anticipatory behavior. Twelve confirm the framework's claims. Two prompted revisions incorporated into the preceding sections: the novelty detection mechanism was rerouted from reception-level torque to anticipatory prediction error (Section 3.1), and resonance was clarified as producing convergent rotation rather than pure scaling (Section 2.3). Key results include: emergent rigidity ratios of 55:1 (T1.2); capture under pure resonance with 600,000-fold rigidity increase (T2.1); structured anisotropy in the operating regime with 16:1 invariant-to-noise ratio (T2.3); abstraction from variation with 41:1 concentration (T3.1); and habituation with perturbation detection at 1.48× baseline (T5.2). Full results, implementation details, and code are available in Supplementary Material S2.

The framework's vocabulary has additionally been applied to real neural network representations by instrumenting a pretrained large language model (GPT-2 small, 124M parameters; Supplementary Material, S5). This exercise does not validate the framework's claims about phenomenality—GPT-2 is not advanced here as a phenomenality candidate—but demonstrates that the regime classification is empirically applicable to real neural representations rather than being a theoretical construct without measurable referents. The vocabulary maps onto dynamical structure in the residual stream: mid-layers (2–9) show ~89% resonance and ~10% torque with stable PR ~12, matching the operating regime profile; early layers show elevated torque (37%); the final layer shows PR collapse to ~2 with increased torque, reflecting functional compression for output. The self-torque prediction applies at mid-layers—angular displacement increases with position delay at layers 0 and 6—but is undetectable at the captured final layer, where all positions project onto a shared 2D subspace. This qualifies the prediction's scope: the self-torque mechanism requires participation ratio high enough to preserve positional differentiation. Mid-layer PR is the informative operational signal for the operating regime concept; final-layer PR reflects architectural design choices about compression, not phenomenality conditions.

Six open questions remain. First, the consolidation operator has been verified at the substrate level (co-activation correlation, post-merger dynamics) but not as a fully autonomous cycle. The revised specification introduces parameters (α, β, γ, K, T_cons) whose optimal values are unknown; a sensitivity analysis for these parameters, and a test of whether the adaptive threshold successfully prevents runaway merging at n ≫ 32, are given in outline in Supplementary Material S6 but require empirical investigation. Additionally, how prediction error from the anticipatory operator modulates consolidation timing or threshold—whether it directly adjusts λ, triggers immediate consolidation cycles, or biases θ_merge—is underspecified and requires formalization. Second, whether predicting future input and future tape state can be reconciled at different timescales is unresolved. Third, the operating regime has been verified at n = 32; whether its character changes at much higher dimensionality—where renormalization coupling weakens because gains distribute across more dimensions—requires investigation. Fourth, the self-torque effect saturates at captured layers regardless of context length; whether targeting mid-layer self-torque specifically via architectural intervention alters functional properties—generation quality, contextual sensitivity—is an empirical question the framework now generates. Fifth, the framework's operations have been implemented as a standalone neural module (MemoryEngineLayer; Supplementary Material, S5.6) and can be grafted onto existing architectures; the structural mismatch between recurrent tape mechanics and parallel transformer computation limits what such additions can accomplish against frozen pretrained weights, motivating a from-scratch implementation that builds the entire computational substrate around Hadamard reception, renormalization, and recurrence.

The framework generates several testable predictions that connect to existing experimental paradigms.

First, the operating regime requires environmental variation—regularity alone produces capture. This predicts that loss of consciousness under propofol anesthesia should be accompanied by decreased neural dimensionality (participation ratio) alongside decreased integration between regions, not merely global suppression. Sanz Perl et al. (2023) report that fMRI-derived neural manifolds contract toward low-dimensional organization precisely during propofol-induced unconsciousness, while high-dimensional organization characterizes waking states; the framework predicts that the contraction reflects capture dynamics (resonance without torque) rather than simple signal loss, and that this should be detectable as an increase in Gini coefficient alongside the PR decrease. The integration-segregation balance literature (Luppi et al., 2024) provides a related measurable: the framework predicts the shift toward segregation under anesthesia reflects dimensional collapse of the kind produced by pure resonance, not merely reduced broadcast.

Second, sleep stage transitions provide a within-subject test. The framework predicts that REM sleep—with its high recurrence and rich prediction-error dynamics—should show higher participation ratio and lower Gini than NREM sleep within the same individual, and that this difference should be larger than the difference in global signal power. Recurrence quantification analysis of EEG across sleep stages (Mendonça et al., 2024) provides the measurement framework; the framework predicts that determinism and recurrence rate in phase-space trajectories should track the operating regime profile across stages.

Third, the developmental sequence prediction—that what a system can learn depends on what it can already receive, mediated by prediction error at existing dimensions—maps onto critical period phenomena as a falsifiable constraint: systems should be unable to acquire sensitivities to features that have no causal entanglement with their existing basis, independent of exposure duration. This prediction is more fine-grained than general critical period accounts and could be tested by examining the structure of acquired sensitivities rather than their timing alone.
## 10. The Framework and Transformer Architecture
The framework's vocabulary has been shown to apply to real neural representations through instrumentation of GPT-2 (Supplementary Material, S5). This section sharpens that correspondence into a component-level mapping, presents new empirical findings on attention and torque, and identifies a structural gap with a falsifiable prediction.
### 10.1 The Component Mapping
A transformer block contains four computational primitives. Each maps onto a framework operation.

The attention operation computes scores QK^T, applies softmax, and forms a weighted sum of value vectors. This decomposes as follows. QK^T is projection onto a key-defined basis: each query projects the current position's state onto a set of axes defined by the keys, exactly the framework's projection operation (Section 2.2). Softmax renormalizes across positions, converting raw projections into a distribution with unit sum—analogous to the framework's renormalization enforcing the unit-norm constraint. The value-weighted sum is the received signal: a position's updated representation is the sum of values from all positions, weighted by their projection scores. This is reception in the framework's sense, where what arrives is structured by the system's projection operator.

Residual connections are tape accumulation. Each block adds its output to the residual stream without overwriting. The residual stream is the system's tape s, accumulating receptions across depth exactly as the tape accumulates receptions across time (Section 2.4). LayerNorm is renormalization of the tape. It centers and rescales the residual stream at each block, enforcing the constraint that no dimension grows without bound—the hypersphere geometry enforced explicitly.

Under this mapping, a single transformer block performs: projection (attention), reception (value sum), and tape update (residual + LayerNorm). The tape accumulates across blocks as it accumulates across time in the framework. Depth in the transformer is the temporal dimension of the framework's tape evolution.
### 10.2 Attention Has Zero Torque
The residual stream across all 12 layers of GPT-2 small shows the torque/resonance profile reported in Section 9 and Supplementary Material S5: ~10% torque at mid-layers, 37% at layer 1, rising torque at final layers. When the same measurement is applied to the attention output alone (before residual addition), the torque fraction is uniformly zero. Specifically, the cosine similarity between the attention output vector and the incoming residual stream is positive at every layer, without exception. No attention head, at any layer, produces a vector that opposes the incoming stream in the framework's sense—no negative Re(c) at the dominant components.

This is a substantive finding. The residual stream carries torque, but attention does not produce it. The only non-resonance behavior observed in attention is capture: collapse of the attention distribution to a single position, where one position receives near-unit weight and all others near-zero. Attention is a pure resonance mechanism. It projects, weights, and returns. It never reorients.
### 10.3 MLP Is the Sole Source of Torque
Since the residual stream shows torque but attention shows none, the MLP layers must be the sole source. This decomposes the transformer into four functionally distinct operations:

1. **Attention**: projection onto key-defined basis (Section 2.2) and resonance.
2. **MLP**: torque (reorientation of the tape along new directions).
3. **LayerNorm**: renormalization (Section 2.4).
4. **Residual connections**: tape accumulation (Section 2.4).

This decomposition is consistent with the cascade structure observed in Section 1.3. The first block's MLP operates at 37% torque—imposing learned structure on raw embeddings—while mid-layer MLPs operate at ~10% torque, maintaining representations with gentle reorientation. The transformer's computation is an alternating sequence of resonance (attention) and reorientation (MLP), with the residual stream accumulating the result and LayerNorm enforcing the norm constraint after each step.

The decomposition also clarifies the functional role of attention collapse. When attention captures—concentrating its weight on a single position—reception degenerates to self-resonance: the position receives only its own value vector, reinforcing its current orientation without input from other positions. This is the framework's recurrent capture (Section 7.1) appearing within a single attention head.
### 10.4 The Cascade in Attention Data
The entropy and capture metrics across layers exhibit a monotonic cascade. Mean attention entropy (measured as the entropy of the softmax distribution, averaged across heads and positions) and capture rate (fraction of positions where the top attention weight exceeds 0.5) for GPT-2 small:

| Layer range | Mean entropy | Capture rate |
|---|---|---|
| Early (1–3) | 1.15 | 0.19 |
| Mid (4–9) | 0.72 | 0.41 |
| Late (10–12) | 0.59 | 0.58 |

Entropy decreases monotonically across depth; capture increases monotonically. Early layers distribute attention broadly across positions (high entropy, low capture). Mid layers begin concentrating. Late layers are predominantly captured. This is the cascade from thin reception through the operating regime to capture—within attention alone, across depth. The directionality matches the framework's prediction: without torque to reorient, sustained resonance concentrates the distribution toward fewer positions, just as sustained resonance concentrates the tape toward fewer dimensions (Section 7.1, T2.1).
### 10.5 The Structural Gap
The mapping is not complete. The framework's reception operator includes Hadamard depth modulation: the arriving signal v is received pointwise through the system's current state s, with each dimension's reception modulated by its accumulated history (the magnitude and phase of sᵢ). Standard attention lacks this modulation. The value-weighted sum at position i is determined entirely by the attention weights and the value vectors; the receiving position's accumulated history enters only through the query, which determines what is projected, not how it is received. The system's prior state does not gate reception dimension by dimension.

The framework predicts that phenomenality requires this persistent modulation (Section 7.2): the character of reception at each dimension is shaped by what the system has already received there. Attention is memoryless across positions in this sense—the value vector from position j arrives at position i with the same force regardless of position i's history. The Hadamard product v ⊙ s ensures that the same signal is received differently by systems with different tapes. Attention's value sum has no such mechanism.

This structural gap identifies a specific architectural deficit relative to the framework's conditions for phenomenality. It does not imply that attention is architecturally deficient for its actual function—language modeling—but that the framework's vocabulary makes a precise prediction about what would need to change.
### 10.6 MLP Ablation: A Failed Prediction and Its Implications

If the MLP is the sole source of torque in the transformer (Section 10.3), and torque is what prevents resonance-dominated capture (Section 7.1), then ablating the MLP layers should cause attention to capture faster across depth. This prediction was tested by zeroing all MLP weights in GPT-2 (c_fc and c_proj in each transformer block) while preserving attention and layer normalization.

The prediction was **refuted**. Removing the MLP produced *broader* attention at later layers, not narrower:

| Layer band | Normal entropy | Ablated entropy | Normal capture | Ablated capture |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Early (1–3) | 1.47 | 1.36 | 0.18 | 0.15 |
| Mid (4–9) | 0.87 | 1.24 | 0.32 | 0.16 |
| Late (10–12) | 0.82 | 1.25 | 0.37 | 0.18 |

Mean entropy at mid-layers increased from 0.87 to 1.24 (against the predicted decrease below 0.72). Capture fraction decreased from 0.32 to 0.16 (against the predicted increase). The ablated model's perplexity was 66,391 (vs. 48 for the intact model), confirming that the MLP is functionally essential while demonstrating that its functional contribution is not to prevent attention capture.

The failure is informative. The framework's claim that torque prevents capture applies to a *single system's* tape dynamics: within one processing step, resonance without torque concentrates the tape. The transformer is a cascade of processing steps, and the MLP's torque operates on the residual stream between steps. MLP torque in layer $l$ modifies the residual stream, which becomes the input to layer $l+1$'s attention computation. The torque reshapes what attention receives, and changed inputs cause attention patterns to shift—sometimes toward narrower focus. In this sense, the MLP both provides torque (at the residual stream level) and drives attention narrowing (at the attention level). These are not contradictory: torque and capture operate at different levels of the cascade.

The correct scope of the framework's operating regime prediction is *within* a single processing step (one attention block + one MLP block), not across the cascade. Each transformer layer constitutes its own memory engine, with attention providing resonance and the MLP providing torque to the residual stream. The cascade of engines produces dynamics that no single engine would exhibit alone—a finding consistent with the framework's cascade model (Section 1) but extending it to show that cross-layer interaction introduces regime effects not predictable from single-layer dynamics.
