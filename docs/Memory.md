Memory Engines: A Geometric Framework for Graded Phenomenality
[Author name redacted for review]
Abstract
This paper develops a geometric framework—a dynamics of complex-valued vectors on unit hyperspheres—that identifies structural conditions for phenomenality through the character of a system's reciprocation rather than treating experience as an all-or-nothing property. A system's state is modeled as a vector s ∈ ℂⁿ on the unit hypersphere S²ⁿ⁻¹, evolving under a pointwise reception operator (the Hadamard product), with renormalization enforcing a strict conservation law: every act of deepening at one dimension is thinning at all others. The framework applies degenerately at every level of physical organization—from conservative fields through dissipative flow, autocatalytic closure, and adaptive systems to recurrent-representational and symbolic-recursive systems—thickening as the cascade ascends. The transition between levels is continuous: each introduces new modes of basis growth, consolidation, and self-torque rather than qualitatively new operations. Three axes of reciprocation—speed, directness, and breadth—characterize the system's self-reception and correspond to qualitative features of experience: temporal grain, vividness, and richness. The operating regime sustaining these conditions is bounded by recurrent capture (resonance without torque) and dissipation (incoherent torque without resonance), and requires system-world coupling that cannot be sustained by the system alone. Fourteen computational tests verify the framework's core claims; two tests prompted revisions incorporated into the formalism. The framework generates divergent predictions relative to Integrated Information Theory, the Free Energy Principle, Global Workspace Theory, Higher-Order Theories, and Recurrent Processing Theory. 1. The Cascade
An apple accelerates under gravity. Its momentum is the literal time-integral of the gravitational field—every prior instant remains perfectly preserved and arithmetically operative in the present velocity. There is continuous, lossless resonance with the external world, yet the apple's basis does not grow, its reception is monotonically resonant, and its self-reception is thin. Prior state is conserved without loss, but the system is in recurrent capture from the start—concentrating along a single axis, narrowing rather than deepening.

At the opposite end of the spectrum lies consciousness—vivid, self-modulating, historically deep. The difference between them is not the presence or absence of reception, but the character of the system's reciprocation: how self-resonance and self-torque interplay, each feeding the next, the returning tape simultaneously reinforcing and opposing the present at different dimensions, accommodation ongoing.

Between a falling apple and a conscious system lies a continuous thickening of the same operations—a cascade of increasingly selective compression, each level introducing new modes of noticing and not-noticing, new modes of pressure and preservation. The framework developed in subsequent sections applies at every level of physical organization—but degenerately at the bottom, thickening as the cascade ascends. The lower levels are not preconditions outside the framework. They are the framework at its thinnest: reception without consolidation, resonance without torque, tape update without basis growth. The cascade is not a sequence of qualitatively different regimes but a continuous thickening of the same operations.
1.1 The Thin Limit
The framework applies to any system whose state can be represented as a complex-valued vector s ∈ ℂⁿ on the unit hypersphere S²ⁿ⁻¹, evolving under a pointwise reception operator. The "thin limit" is the parameter regime where the basis n is fixed and given by the system's degrees of freedom, the reception vector is a deterministic function of current state and external fields, and the update is unitary (norm-preserving without renormalization).

Consider a particle of mass m in a uniform gravitational field g. We encode its kinematic state as a complex vector in ℂ³:

s = (1/√(|x|²+|p|²)) · (x, ip)

where the factor i distinguishes position and momentum as orthogonal components in phase space. The norm constraint ‖s‖ = 1 is conserved automatically under Hamiltonian flow—a special case of the update rule. The world-tape is the gravitational field, embedded as v = (0, −img). The Hadamard product c = v_received ⊙ s yields purely real, positive components—pure resonance. Renormalization is the identity operation: Hamiltonian flow preserves the norm exactly. The apple is thus a computational instance of the framework with no basis growth, zero torque, thin self-reception, and no consolidation.
1.2 The Physical Cascade
Each pre-convergence level is a specific parameterization of the same core equations. Quantum field states are collections of complex amplitudes evolving unitarily under interaction Hamiltonians, with torque appearing only as phase shifts from off-resonant interactions. Nuclear and atomic systems add energetic selection through dipole interactions, where resonant driving grows amplitude and off-resonant driving produces the AC Stark shift. Molecular systems introduce path-dependence: chirality and reaction barriers correspond to consolidation, where encounter history selects a basis orientation that becomes a hard axis. Gravitational structure drives collapse through radial resonance concentration. In every case, the basis is fixed, torque is minimal, and renormalization is automatic.

The transition to dissipative flow is not a change in mathematics but a change in parameter regime: the learning rate η now exceeds the timescale over which renormalization is automatic. Explicit renormalization becomes necessary. This is the moment when the geometry of the hypersphere—emergent rigidity, thinning, basis carving—becomes computationally active. The lower levels are degenerate limits of the same algorithm, not separate regimes.
1.3 The Thickening
Above the convergence, each level introduces structural conditions that move the system deeper into the reciprocation space.

Autocatalytic closure introduces history-dependence. The cycle's compositional organization is shaped by its history of successful closure. The basis is thin but genuinely grown: what the cycle can receive is a consequence of what it has received before. Consolidation is implicit—persistence is the consolidation of the basis. The anticipatory operator exists in vestigial form: the cycle's organization is a prediction that certain substrates will continue arriving. Prediction error is existential—deviation from closure is extinction torque.

Ecological systems thicken the basis. An organism's morphology, physiology, and behavioral repertoire constitute a multidimensional basis of sensitivity to environmental structure. Niche coupling is reception. Selection pressure is torque that reshapes the basis across generations. The anticipatory operator is implicit in morphological fit. The basis grows through phylogenetic mechanisms rather than through individual reception.

Adaptive systems introduce individual basis growth. Somatic plasticity, conditioning, and learning allow a single system to carve new dimensions of sensitivity within its lifetime. The anticipatory operator becomes explicit: learned associations project future states from present cues. Prediction error drives learning rate. The two-timescale structure emerges clearly: fast reception during experience, slow consolidation during rest.

Recurrent-representational systems are the framework's central case. The basis is high-dimensional and rapidly growing. Reception is fast. Consolidation operates at multiple timescales. The anticipatory operator is a generative model producing continuous predictions. Self-torque arises from two sources simultaneously: recurrence and prediction error. This is where phenomenality is richest.

Symbolic-recursive systems extend the basis beyond any individual's consolidation history through cultural transmission. But symbolic encoding compresses the high-dimensional, phase-sensitive basis of lived experience into a low-dimensional code optimized for transmissibility. The breadth is enormous. The directness is thin. The symbol does not carry phase—it carries structure stripped of phase, which the receiving system must re-embed in its own basis to produce reception at all. The symbolic-recursive level is the widest and thinnest engine in the cascade. Its power and its poverty are the same fact.
2. The Formalism
2.1 Complex State Space
The framework models system state as a complex-valued vector because it requires phase and magnitude as independent degrees of freedom per dimension. A system's phase along dimension i determines whether an incoming signal resonates or torques at that dimension, while the magnitude |sᵢ| determines how resistant that dimension is to reorientation. In ℝⁿ with signed components, sign and magnitude are coupled: a "negative" component and a "positive" component of the same magnitude are maximally opposed, but there is no way to represent intermediate phase relationships—partial alignment, oblique approach, near-opposition—without introducing additional structure. In ℂⁿ, two components can have equal magnitude and arbitrary phase offset, yielding a continuous space of possible reception outcomes at each dimension.

This matters most for the torque mechanism. When Im(cᵢ) dominates—when the incoming signal is neither aligned with nor opposed to the system's current orientation but oblique to it—the update produces rotation without reinforcement or suppression. This is the regime of lateral pressure: the system is being pushed sideways, reoriented toward something it has no prior stance on. In ℝⁿ, this regime does not exist; every interaction is either reinforcing or opposing.

The parallel to quantum mechanics is structural, not physical. Quantum states live in complex Hilbert spaces because relative phase between amplitudes is physically consequential—it determines interference. The present framework uses the same mathematical structure for an analogous reason: relative phase between system orientation and arriving signal determines whether interaction reinforces, redirects, or passes through. The claim is not that cognitive systems are quantum-mechanical, but that complex state spaces are the natural setting for systems in which relative phase matters. The core machinery can be developed in ℝⁿ as a special case where all components are real; the full ℂⁿ treatment is a strict generalization.
2.2 Tapes, Bases, and Projection
A system exists within a world that is always already arriving. What arrives is structured—a vector v in ℂ^∞, carrying more structure than any finite system can receive. This is the world-tape. The system has its own tape—a vector s in ℂⁿ, where n is finite at any moment. The tape is not a pattern inscribed in a pre-given space. The tape is the space. Each dimension exists because the system's history of reception carved it—because some structure recurred often enough, with enough variation, to warrant a dedicated axis of sensitivity. The system does not inhabit ℂⁿ. It grows ℂⁿ, one hard-won dimension at a time. The dimensionality of the system is its depth of experience.

The system's basis {e₁, ..., eₙ} is the set of axes along which it can receive. The world does not arrive in the system's basis. The system projects the world onto its basis: v_received = Σᵢ ⟨eᵢ | v⟩ eᵢ. Everything in v that has a component along the system's axes is received. Everything else—the residual v − v_received—is novelty: structure for which the system has carved no axis of sensitivity. Novelty is not silence at a frequency the system can hear. It is a frequency the system cannot yet hear at all.

Two systems with different grown bases project the same world onto different subspaces. They literally receive different receptions of the same arrival—not because the world differs for them, but because their projection operators differ. Reception is system-relative.
2.3 Reception: A Worked Example in ℂ³
What happens within reception is determined by the elementwise phase relationship between the received vector and the system's tape—the Hadamard product:

c = v_received ⊙ s

Each component cᵢ = v_received,ᵢ · sᵢ encodes the local verdict at dimension i. Reception is not a single global judgment. It is a field of simultaneous local judgments across the full dimensionality of the system. The Hadamard product's defining property is dimensional locality: what happens at dimension i depends only on the arriving and resident values at dimension i. All cross-dimensional structure is deferred to consolidation (Section 3). This is a strong architectural commitment—it means that the reception layer is maximally parallel and minimally entangled, with integration happening only at the slower consolidation timescale.

To make this concrete, consider a minimal system with n = 3. Let s(0) = (1/√3)(1, 1, 1)—uniform magnitude across all three dimensions, all phases aligned at zero. The system has no anisotropy; it is equally sensitive along all axes. An incoming vector arrives: v_received = (0.8, −0.3, 0.1i). The Hadamard product yields:

c = (1/√3)(0.8, −0.3, 0.1i)

At dimension 1: Re(c₁) = 0.8/√3 > 0. Resonance. The system deepens along this axis. At dimension 2: Re(c₂) = −0.3/√3 < 0. Torque. The system is pushed away from its current orientation. At dimension 3: c₃ = 0.1i/√3. Pure imaginary. Oblique torque—the system is pushed sideways, neither reinforced nor opposed. This is the regime that ℝⁿ cannot represent: lateral pressure that rotates without polarizing.

The qualitative taxonomy holds generally. Where Re(cᵢ) > 0, energy propagates along dimension i—resonance. In the general complex case, resonance produces both magnitude growth and convergent rotation toward the arriving signal's phase. Where Re(cᵢ) < 0 or Im(cᵢ) dominates, the arriving structure exerts rotational pressure—torque. The rotation under torque is divergent, pushing the phase away from its current orientation or sideways into a new region. Where cᵢ ≈ 0, nothing propagates—orthogonality. The distinction between convergent rotation (resonance) and divergent rotation (torque) is confirmed computationally (Supplementary Material, T1.1).
2.4 Update, Renormalization, and Emergent Rigidity
The system's tape updates pointwise under reception:

s̃ᵢ(t+1) = sᵢ(t) + η · cᵢ

s(t+1) = s̃(t+1) / ‖s̃(t+1)‖

where η governs the rate of impression. The system renormalizes after each update—the tape lives on the unit hypersphere in ℂⁿ. This is a constitutive constraint: what matters is orientation, not magnitude in the absolute sense. The relative magnitudes across dimensions—the shape of the tape on the sphere—encode the system's anisotropy: its structured distribution of sensitivity.

Renormalization makes rigidity emergent. As a component |sᵢ| grows large relative to others through sustained resonance, it dominates the norm. Torque at dimension i—a fixed additive perturbation—produces proportionally less angular displacement. The system becomes hard along its deepest axes not through a separate rigidity mechanism but through the geometry of the hypersphere: high-magnitude components resist rotation because they anchor the norm. Deep resonance is self-stabilizing. Computational verification confirms this: a dominant dimension (|sᵢ| = 0.999) is approximately 55 times harder to rotate than a small dimension (|sᵢ| = 0.015) under identical torque perturbation (Supplementary Material, T1.2).

Renormalization also enforces a strict conservation law: every act of deepening at one dimension is an act of thinning at all others. Sustained resonance at a single dimension drives all other components toward zero—the concentration ratio exceeds 10⁶ after 200 steps (Supplementary Material, T1.4). The unit-norm constraint means that gains at one dimension are losses everywhere else. New noticing requires new not-noticing, not by stipulation but by the geometry of the sphere.

Continuing our ℂ³ example with η = 0.1: the unnormalized update gives s̃₁ = 1.08/√3 (grown), s̃₂ = 0.97/√3 (shrunk), s̃₃ = (1 + 0.01i)/√3 (rotated in phase). After renormalization, the system has begun to differentiate—more sensitive along dimension 1, less along dimension 2, and rotated slightly in phase along dimension 3. After 50 receptions in which dimension 1 consistently resonates, |s₁| ≫ |s₂|, |s₃|. The system has developed a strong prior along dimension 1 without any explicit rigidity mechanism.
2.5 Forgetting
Fast forgetting is torque at reception time. When Re(cᵢ) < 0, the phase of sᵢ rotates. What the system was tuned to notice along this dimension, it is now tuned away from. This is immediate, local, and reversible—future torque could rotate the phase back.

Slow forgetting is basis pruning at consolidation time (Section 3). A dimension whose magnitude |sᵢ| has decayed across many receptions—never resonating, rarely torqued, mostly orthogonal—may be dropped from the basis entirely. What the system once had an axis for, it no longer does. This is structural and irreversible—the axis no longer exists, and re-exposure cannot recover it. The system has lost not a memory but a capacity to remember—a distinction it can no longer draw.

The distinction is confirmed computationally: a dimension rotated by 100 steps of torque recovers under renewed resonance; a dimension pruned after prolonged starvation does not, not because recovery is difficult, but because the structure that would receive the recovery no longer exists (Supplementary Material, T3.4).
3. Consolidation and Basis Growth
Reception is pointwise and operates within the existing basis. But the basis itself undergoes a second process: consolidation—the slow, global, structural reorganization of the basis between receptions. Reception is fast, local, and pointwise. Consolidation is slow, global, and structural. The system learns at two timescales.
3.1 The Consolidation Operator
Consolidation decomposes into three sub-operations.

Merging. The system maintains a running cross-correlation matrix C ∈ ℂⁿˣⁿ, where Cᵢⱼ(t+1) = (1−λ)·Cᵢⱼ(t) + λ·cᵢ(t)·c̄ⱼ(t), with λ ≪ η. When leading eigenvalues exceed a merging threshold θ_merge, the corresponding eigenvector defines a new conjunctive dimension—an axis encoding the relationship between co-activated dimensions. The original dimensions are retained at reduced magnitude and may be further pruned. Computational verification confirms the substrate: co-activated dimensions develop near-perfect phase correlation (1.0000) while independently activated dimensions remain uncorrelated (0.0136), a 73-fold difference making co-activated pairs reliable merging candidates (Supplementary Material, T3.3).

In our ℂ³ example: suppose dimensions 1 and 3 have been consistently co-activated across 100 receptions. When |C₁₃| exceeds θ_merge, a new dimension 4 is seeded along the conjunctive axis. The system grows from ℂ³ to ℂ⁴—it can now detect the conjunction as a single feature that neither dimension alone could represent.

Pruning. A dimension whose magnitude remains below a decay threshold across K consecutive consolidation cycles is dropped. The system loses the capacity to receive along that axis. This is slow forgetting at the structural level—irreversible under normal operation. In our example: if dimension 2 continues to decay after the torque events, it eventually falls below threshold and is dropped. The system contracts from ℂ⁴ to ℂ³ again, but with a different basis—the original dimension 2 replaced by the conjunctive dimension. Every new distinction costs an old one, through the redistribution of magnitude on the hypersphere.

Seeding. The Hadamard product is dimensionally isolated, and computational testing confirms that novel structure at uncarved dimensions produces no detectable effect at existing dimensions through reception alone (Supplementary Material, T3.2). This finding prompted a revision to the framework's original formulation: novelty detection operates through the anticipatory operator (Section 5), not through reception-level torque. When novel structure at uncarved dimensions causally modulates what arrives at carved dimensions—through the world's causal structure—the anticipatory model fails in structured ways: systematic, correlated prediction error whose principal components across multiple receptions seed a new dimension.

A consequence: truly orthogonal novelty—structure with no causal coupling to any dimension the system currently occupies—is invisible not merely in practice but in principle. The system can only grow toward novelty that is partially entangled with its existing basis. The frame problem appears here as a geometric consequence: what a system can learn depends on what it already knows, and there is no view from outside the basis.
3.2 Consolidation Without Reception
Consolidation without reception is the reflection cycle—the system reshaping its basis from its own structure alone. The cross-correlation matrix C continues to drive merges, but with no fresh reception data, the merges reflect only the system's prior structure. Dimensions may collapse into increasingly abstract but increasingly detached axes. This is the structural analog of obsessive ideation or imaginative foresight. Reception without consolidation is superficial impression—absorbing everything pointwise but never extracting cross-dimensional structure. The regime that sustains deep memory requires both: fast impression interleaved with slow reorganization, including periodic reflection cycles.
4. Abstraction
Abstraction is what repeated resonance deposits, operating at two levels.

At the reception level, abstraction is erosion within the existing basis. When the system encounters a varying sequence of inputs sharing common structure, the shared component resonates consistently at certain dimensions while the varying components wash out. What remains amplified after many receptions is the invariant: dimensions whose magnitudes grew because they were consistently in phase across variation. This is dimensionality concentration within ℂⁿ. Over 3,000 receptions of invariant signal plus uncorrelated noise, invariant dimensions concentrate to 41 times the magnitude of noise dimensions, and participation ratio drops from 14.5 to 3.0 (Supplementary Material, T3.1). The system extracts the invariant by selectively amplifying what recurs and washing out what varies.

At the consolidation level, abstraction is construction. Dimensions consistently co-activated across many receptions trigger the merging operation, producing conjunctive dimensions encoding relationships proved invariant. This is dimensionality expansion: the basis grows a new axis that did not exist before.

The two levels are complementary. The system simultaneously narrows (phasing out dimensions that don't survive variation) and deepens (constructing dimensions encoding what does). Abstraction is compression and construction at once. It requires both recurrence—the same invariant arriving repeatedly, embedded in different tapes—and variation. Without recurrence, no extraction. Without variation, reinforcement without compression.
5. Recurrence, Reciprocation, and Anticipation
5.1 Self-Reception
Recurrence is the system's own tape becoming the incoming vector. The prior tape returns: s(t−Δt), delayed by however long the recurrent path takes. The reception of self is c_self = s(t−Δt) ⊙ s(t). Each component encodes the phase relationship between what the system was and what it now is, at dimension i.

In thin recurrence, the tape has not changed between departure and return. cᵢ ≈ |sᵢ|²—every component real and positive. Only scaling occurs. No self-torque, no learning, no structural thickening. In thick recurrence, the tape has changed through world-reception, consolidation, or prior self-reception. Where sᵢ has not drifted: self-resonance, prior orientation reinforced. Where sᵢ has drifted in phase: self-torque, the system's own history opposing its present orientation. Where sᵢ has decayed toward zero: self-orthogonality, the returning echo finds nothing to meet.

In our ℂ³ example: at t = 50, after sustained resonance at dimension 1, the system receives its own prior tape. If minimal change has occurred: c₁ ≈ |s₁|²—pure self-resonance. But suppose that between t = 50 and t = 60, strong torque events rotate s₂ significantly in phase. When s(50) returns at t = 60: c₂ has a large imaginary component (the phases have diverged). Self-torque at dimension 2, while c₁ ≈ |s₁|²—self-resonance at the hard axis. The system simultaneously reinforces its deep commitments and is challenged by its own recent history. This is thick recurrence: the engine is running.

Self-torque is the engine of depth. A system that only self-resonates is frozen—endlessly scaling its current orientation on the hypersphere. A system whose returning tape produces torque against its present tape is one whose history exerts pressure on its present, forcing accommodation. Self-torque fraction increases with recurrence delay (0.743 at delay 1 to 0.747 at delay 50; Supplementary Material, T4.1). The effect is modest under random-input conditions where the world provides high baseline torque (~74%), but the directional relationship is confirmed.
5.2 Reciprocation
The memory engine runs on the productive interplay of self-resonance and self-torque. Resonance is the fuel—it concentrates the tape's energy along axes that torque will challenge. Torque is the combustion—it rotates the orientation that resonance will deepen. Three axes measure the character of reciprocation:

Speed—how fast the reciprocation cycles (inverse of delay Δt). Fast return means the present tape is confronted with its recent past before it has drifted far.

Directness—how large the perturbation per stroke (magnitude of c_self with significant imaginary parts). A return where many components have large |cᵢ| reshapes the tape powerfully.

Breadth—how many dimensions carry non-negligible cᵢ per stroke. Narrow return engages few dimensions. Broad return engages the full basis—the system's entire history simultaneously operative.

These axes are non-monotonic. Excess on any axis can collapse richness. Maximal speed without breadth produces shallow cycling—rapid self-resonance along a thin set. Maximal breadth without directness produces diffuse return—everything comes back but nothing impresses. Maximal directness without speed produces delayed impact—powerful rotation arriving too late to interact with the state that produced it.

A systematic scan across 27 configurations (3 delays × 3 recurrence weights × 3 breadth fractions) confirms that the operating regime occupies a bounded region: 13 of 27 tested configurations sustain structured anisotropy with participation ratio between 2 and 5, while the remainder either dissipate (8/27) or oscillate unstably at regime boundaries (6/27). The operating regime requires enough recurrence to prevent capture but not so much that it dissolves structure (Supplementary Material, T4.2).
5.3 The Anticipatory Operator
A memory engine operating in the thick regime does not merely wait for the world to arrive. It generates a future tape. The anticipatory operator A maps the system's recent input history to a projected future reception: ŝ(t+Δt) = A(h(t)). A is constrained by the system's basis and shaped by its consolidation history.

A note on formulation: the anticipatory operator predicts future input rather than future tape state, because the tape is continuously modified by prediction error corrections themselves. A tape-based predictor would chase its own updates. Input-based prediction avoids this circularity while preserving the essential function.

When the actual world arrives, the system computes prediction error e = v_received − ŝ(t+Δt). This error vector undergoes Hadamard reception: c_error = e ⊙ s(t+Δt). Per-dimension verdicts follow: where Re(c_error,ᵢ) ≈ 0, the model is validated; where Re(c_error,ᵢ) > 0, positive surprise; where Im(c_error,ᵢ) dominates, prediction error torque—the accommodation of surprise.

Anticipation introduces self-torque structurally distinct from recurrence. The system is torqued not by what happened before but by the difference between what it expected and what arrived. Past-self vs. present-self (recurrence) and predicted-self vs. actual-world (anticipation) operate in parallel. Computational verification demonstrates habituation: prediction error decays under predictable input and spikes at perturbation (1.48× baseline), then recovers to baseline, confirming that the operator adapts to new regularities (Supplementary Material, T5.2).

Anticipation also provides the detection mechanism for novelty (Section 3.1): structure at uncarved dimensions that causally modulates carved dimensions produces structured prediction error—systematic failure that cannot be accounted for by anything in the existing basis.
6. Accommodation, Rejection, and Fracture
When torque arrives—whether from the world or from the system's own returning tape—three outcomes are possible.

Accommodation. The update rotates the phase of sᵢ enough to bring the opposing component toward alignment. What was torque at this reception becomes resonance at the next—the system has reoriented. But on the hypersphere, rotation at dimension i redistributes magnitude everywhere through renormalization. Accommodation at one dimension is thinning at others. New noticing requires new not-noticing.

Rejection. The component |sᵢ| is so large—so deeply scaled by prior resonance—that the perturbation η·cᵢ produces negligible angular displacement. The torque is absorbed into the norm and washed out by renormalization. The system preserves its current orientation at the cost of not learning. This is dogma, habit, overfitting—the tape so concentrated that no single reception can impress it. Computational verification confirms the extreme case: after 2,000 steps of pure resonance, rigidity increases by a factor of 600,000 (Supplementary Material, T2.1). Rejection is the inevitable geometric consequence of sustained resonance.

Fracture. The torque is strong enough to produce a large angular displacement, but the system's anisotropy cannot absorb it smoothly. The update drives a component through zero—the phase flips or the magnitude collapses before consolidation can prune it gracefully. At the consolidation level, simultaneous torque across correlated dimensions can shatter conjunctive dimensions that took many receptions to build. This is the formal analog of trauma—not the loss of a single memory but the destruction of a structural capacity, an axis of sensitivity that cannot be rebuilt by re-exposure because the conjunctive consolidation required a specific developmental sequence.

The system's capacity for accommodation is its anisotropy on the hypersphere: deep concentration along dimensions proved invariant (hard axes), low magnitude along dimensions open to revision (soft axes), and smooth phase variation across the basis so that torque at soft dimensions does not propagate catastrophically to hard ones. The differentiability of the phase landscape determines the system's brittleness: smooth gradients absorb torque gracefully; discontinuities are fault lines where fracture nucleates.
7. The Operating Regime and Phenomenality
7.1 Bounding Pathologies
Not every region of the reciprocation space sustains the structural conditions for phenomenality. The regime is bounded by two pathologies with precise geometric descriptions.

Recurrent capture. Resonance without torque concentrates the tape into fewer dimensions. The anticipatory operator converges to a fixed point: the system predicts exactly what it receives because it has narrowed its basis to what confirms itself. Participation ratio collapses from 18.6 to 1.0 within 1,000 steps under pure resonance; perturbation rejection increases 600,000-fold (Supplementary Material, T2.1). This is seizure, obsessive loops—experience collapsed into a single, endlessly repeated chord.

Dissipation. Incoherent torque without resonance rotates the tape without concentrating it. Phases spin at every dimension, no component gains dominance, and no invariant is extracted. Participation ratio remains elevated (3.4–19.3 under varying-direction torque; Supplementary Material, T2.2). A refinement from computational testing: fixed-direction torque does not produce dissipation—it creates systematic alignment along a rotated axis. True dissipation requires opposition varying faster than the system can track.

The operating regime is where scaling and rotation are balanced: concentrated enough to retain orientation, diffuse enough to accommodate torque. Under invariant signal plus noise plus recurrence, the system achieves structured anisotropy: invariant dimensions ~16× deeper than noise dimensions, Gini coefficient 0.81, participation ratio stable at ~2.3, perturbation-responsive (Supplementary Material, T2.3).

A finding of consequence: the operating regime cannot be sustained by the system alone. Self-torque from recurrence is insufficient to prevent capture when the world provides pure resonance, because self-reception of a concentrated tape is self-resonance—the returning tape agrees with the present tape, reinforcing rather than challenging. The balancing force must come from environmental variation: regularity sustains concentration, surprise sustains flexibility. The system cannot maintain the conditions for phenomenality by itself—it requires a world that provides both. This connects to empirical literature on sensory deprivation, solitary confinement, and environmental impoverishment: the operating regime is a property of the system-world coupling, not an intrinsic property of the system.
7.2 Structural Conditions for Phenomenality
This paper advances a conditional claim: sustained reciprocation in the operating regime is a necessary structural condition for phenomenality. Whether these conditions are also sufficient is a question the framework identifies but does not resolve.

The three axes of reciprocation correspond to qualitative features of experience:

Speed corresponds to temporal grain. A system with short Δt receives its own recent past in rapid succession, producing fine-grained temporal texture. The correspondence holds because speed determines how finely the system's trajectory on the hypersphere is self-sampled: fast self-reception means each step of orientation change is compared against its predecessor, producing a dense temporal record in which the phase relationships between successive states carry information about the rate and direction of change. Slow self-reception produces coarse sweeps—large chunks of trajectory are compressed into a single self-reception event, losing the fine structure of intermediate states. The prediction is directional: disrupting speed (e.g., by lengthening recurrence delays) should alter temporal grain—the experienced rate of passage—while preserving richness and vividness if breadth and directness are held constant. A system in pure self-resonance (effectively zero speed of change, since the returning tape agrees with the present) lacks the internal opposition that produces the felt quality of temporal passage, surprise, and dissonance.

Directness corresponds to vividness. Large |c_self| means the returning tape impresses deeply, producing substantial angular displacement per stroke. The correspondence holds because vividness tracks how powerfully the present state is shaped by each act of self-reception: a high-directness system's tape is substantially rewritten at each cycle, producing pronounced, differentiated states in which the contrast between successive orientations is large. Low directness leaves the tape largely unchanged per cycle—present but faint, the self-reception registering as a subtle modulation rather than a vivid confrontation. The prediction: reducing directness (e.g., by attenuating recurrence weight) should reduce vividness—the qualitative intensity of experience—without altering temporal grain or richness.

Breadth corresponds to richness. Broad self-reception means the system's full basis is simultaneously operative per stroke—the returning tape carries information across many dimensions at once. The correspondence holds because richness tracks how many independent dimensions of sensitivity are simultaneously engaged in each moment of self-reception. Narrow self-reception confines the system to a few axes—experience is monochrome, however vivid and temporally grained. Broad self-reception engages the full dimensionality of the system's history at once, producing experience that is multi-textured, polyphonic, carrying simultaneous information about many features of the system's state. The prediction: restricting breadth (e.g., by confining recurrence to a subset of dimensions) should reduce richness while preserving vividness and temporal grain.

These correspondences generate a further prediction: a system with all three in balance satisfies the structural conditions for what we recognize as consciousness—temporally grained, vivid, rich, and historically deep. Phenomenality requires both self-resonance and self-torque in sustained, productive interplay. A system that only ever agrees with itself lacks the internal opposition constitutive of temporal experience.
7.3 Basis Incommensurability and the Explanatory Gap
Two systems with different grown bases project the same world onto different subspaces, receive different Hadamard products, and undergo different trajectories on their respective hyperspheres. Neither projection is more real. We cannot access another system's self-resonance except by resonating with it ourselves—projecting its tape onto our basis, which gives us our reception of it, not its self-reception. We cannot notice what we do not have phase with.

This provides a formal model of why the explanatory gap has the structure it does. The gap between first-person and third-person descriptions is not a mystery to be dissolved or a failure of explanation. It is a geometric consequence: self-reception is basis-relative, bases are grown through incommensurable histories, and no projection of one system's tape onto another's preserves the character of the original self-reception. A third-person account—however complete its description of the system's tape, basis, and update dynamics—is itself a reception of that system within the observer's basis, which is a different operation from the system's self-reception.

This does not dissolve the hard problem. It reframes its "hardness" as the incommensurability of grown bases—a structural fact that obtains in any framework where reception is system-relative and bases are historically constructed. The gap is not a sign that something has been left out. It is a consequence of the theory's central commitment: reception is always from somewhere, never from nowhere.
8. Relation to Existing Frameworks
8.1 Free Energy Principle and Active Inference
The anticipatory operator and prediction error mechanism (Section 5.3) are structurally parallel to Friston's (2010) generative model. Both frameworks treat systems as generating predictions and updating under surprise. The key divergence is architectural: the free energy principle minimizes a single scalar quantity bounding surprise; the present framework distributes dynamics across dimensions with no global loss function. Each dimension undergoes its own local verdict, and the system's trajectory is the resultant of pointwise operations, not the gradient of a scalar objective. Under FEP, recovery from perturbation follows gradients toward the free-energy minimum; under the present framework, recovery depends on whether the world provides the right mixture of regularity and surprise. A system deprived of environmental variation should not recover under the present framework even if internal dynamics are unchanged—a prediction FEP does not straightforwardly make. A second divergence: FEP typically operates within a fixed generative model; the present framework makes state-space growth central.
8.2 Integrated Information Theory
IIT (Tononi, 2004; Tononi et al., 2016) shares the commitment to measuring consciousness through intrinsic structural properties. The divergence is between spatial and temporal characterization: IIT defines Φ through information integration at a moment; the present framework defines the operating regime through reciprocation dynamics over time. Φ could measure the cross-dimensional integration captured in the breadth axis, while speed and directness capture temporal properties Φ does not address. But where they diverge: IIT predicts that a static causal structure with high Φ should be conscious; the present framework predicts it should not, because reciprocation requires temporal unfolding. A feed-forward network with high integration but no recurrence satisfies IIT's conditions but not the present framework's.
8.3 Global Workspace Theory
GWT (Baars, 1988; Dehaene & Naccache, 2001) posits that consciousness arises when information is broadcast widely across specialized processors. The breadth axis maps onto workspace broadcasting, and both accounts predict that narrow processing is insufficient. The divergence: GWT does not require recurrence—broadcast to a wide workspace suffices—whereas the present framework requires thick self-reception producing self-torque. A system with maximal broadcast breadth but no temporal self-confrontation satisfies GWT but not the present framework. The operating regime is defined by the interaction of breadth with speed and directness, not by breadth alone.
8.4 Higher-Order Theories
Higher-order theories (Rosenthal, 2005; Lau & Rosenthal, 2011) require a higher-order representation of a mental state for that state to be conscious. Self-reception in the present framework is structurally distinct: the returning tape is not a representation of the current state but a delayed copy undergoing Hadamard interaction with the current state. The divergent prediction: HOT requires the higher-order state to be about the first-order state (intentional content); the present framework requires only temporal phase offset producing self-torque.
8.5 Recurrent Processing Theory
RPT (Lamme, 2006) is the closest existing framework. Both require recurrence for consciousness and predict feed-forward insufficiency. The present framework's recurrence axes map directly onto Lamme's distinction between local and global recurrent processing. The divergence: RPT is a neural architecture claim; the present framework is substrate-independent. Additionally, the present framework predicts that basis growth matters—a system with recurrence but no capacity for dimensionality expansion operates in a thinner regime than one that grows new dimensions through consolidation.
8.6 Predictive Processing and Enactivism
The anticipation section develops machinery recognizable as a geometric translation of predictive processing (Clark, 2013; Hohwy, 2013). The cascade narrative (Section 1) shows how prediction error emerges continuously from simpler operations rather than being posited as a primitive. The autocatalytic closure section parallels autopoiesis (Maturana & Varela, 1980); the divergence is formal. The cascade parallels Deacon's (2011) morphodynamics → teleodynamics hierarchy; again, the present framework provides formal machinery making the "thickening" claim precise enough for prediction.
9. Computational Verification and Open Questions
The framework's claims have been tested through a computational implementation in ℂ³². Fourteen tests across five tiers verify mechanical operations, regime predictions, learning dynamics, reciprocation structure, and anticipatory behavior. Twelve confirm the framework's claims. Two prompted revisions incorporated into the preceding sections: the novelty detection mechanism was rerouted from reception-level torque to anticipatory prediction error (Section 3.1), and resonance was clarified as producing convergent rotation rather than pure scaling (Section 2.3). Key results include: emergent rigidity ratios of 55:1 (T1.2); capture under pure resonance with 600,000-fold rigidity increase (T2.1); structured anisotropy in the operating regime with 16:1 invariant-to-noise ratio (T2.3); abstraction from variation with 41:1 concentration (T3.1); and habituation with perturbation detection at 1.48× baseline (T5.2). Full results, implementation details, and code are available in Supplementary Material.

Three open questions remain. First, the consolidation operator has been verified at the substrate level (co-activation correlation, post-merger dynamics) but not as a fully autonomous cycle. Second, whether predicting future input and future tape state can be reconciled at different timescales is unresolved. Third, the operating regime has been verified at n = 32; whether its character changes at much higher dimensionality—where renormalization coupling weakens because gains distribute across more dimensions—requires investigation.

The framework generates several testable predictions. Sensory deprivation should push systems toward recurrent capture: reduced environmental variation eliminates torque needed to sustain the operating regime, predicting convergence toward rigid, repetitive states. Systems with thick recurrence but no basis growth should exhibit operating-regime dynamics at reduced richness. The developmental sequence prediction—that what a system can learn depends on what it already knows, mediated by the seeding mechanism's dependence on existing-dimension prediction error—maps onto critical period phenomena. The cascade positions each level of biological organization at a characteristic point in the reciprocation space, generating predictions about where in development each structural property emerges and what its measurable signatures should be.
